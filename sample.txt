वसिष्ठः:
चित्तत्त्वस्य फलस्येव चित्रस् सोम्यो ऽप्य् अनुक्रमात् ।
स्वसत्तासन्निवेशो ऽन्तर् यस् स सर्ग इति स्थितः ॥1॥

Vasis. . t ha:
1 The [as appearance] manifold, although [in reality] unchanged uniform (somya) 1 composition [of its] own being, which [exists] in structured arrangement (anukrama) within the reality of mind (cittattva) like [within] a fruit, is present as "creation".


देशकालक्रियादीनाम् अति तन्मयरूपतः ।
इदम् अन्यद् इदं चान्यद् इति नात्रोपपद्यते ॥2॥

2 Because the form of place, time, activity, etc., consists entirely (ati) of this [reality of mind], [a differentiating statement of the kind] "This [is] one and this another" is not possible in relation to it.


समस्तशब्दशब्दार्थवासनाकल्पनाविदाम् ।
एकात्मत्वाद् अन्यथेदम् इति सङ्कथ्यते कथम् ॥3॥

3 How [could] it be said [that] this [world] [exists] in a different way, because [yet] all sounds, sound meanings, [inner] imprints, conceptualizations and cognitions are one and the same being?


फलस्यान्तस् सन्निवेशो नानानुक्रमतो यथा ।
॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥ ॥4॥

4 As the composition [exists] in a fruit in manifold ways of instructured arrangement. 2


अनानैवापि नानेव क्षुब्धेवाक्षुभितैव च ।
यथा फलान्तस् स्वा सत्ता चिदन्तश् चिच्चयस् तथा ॥5॥

5 Just as in a fruit [its] own being, although [it is] actually uniform, appears to be manifold and seems to be in motion, actually [but] is not in motion, [it is also] the case with the mind-mass (ciccaya) in the mind (cidantar).


जगन्नगरम् आदर्शे चितीव प्रतिबिम्बितम् ।
कचतीवाकचद् अपि शिलान्तस् सन्निवेशवत् ॥6॥

6 The city "world", which is reﬂected as it were in the spirit (cit) as a mirror 3 , seems to shine, although [it itself actually] does not shine, 4 like the structure in a stone 5 .


परमे चिन्मणौ सन्ति जगत्कोटिशतान्य् अपि ।
चिन्तामणाव् अनन्तानि फलानीवार्थितान्य् अलम् ॥7॥

7 In the absolute spirit gem (cinman. i) are even billions ofworlds, as in a desire gem 1 to the sufficiency infinite [many] desired results [are].


चित्समुद्गक एवेदं तदङ्गोत्कीर्णम् आततम् ।
जगन्मौक्तिकम् आभाति तदंशुमयम् अन्यवत् ॥8॥

8 Exclusively in the [shell] shell 2 "spirit" (cit) shines this formed pearl "world" as if [it was something displaced from the shell], although it is worked out of the body of the [shell] [and] consists of its shine.


अहोरात्रान् विरचयन् वेदनावेदनान्य् अयम् ।
चिदादित्यस् स्थितो भास्वाञ् जगद्द्रव्याणि दर्शयन् ॥9॥

9 This luminous spirit-sun (cidāditya) continues (sthita) to bring forth the days and nights, [namely] the realizations and misrealizations, [and] to display the objects of the world.


समुद्रकोटरावर्तपयस्स्पन्दविलासवत् ।
न व्यक्तं नानभिव्यक्तं त्रिजगद् वर्तते चिति ॥10॥

10 The triad of worlds takes [its course] in the mind (cit) neither in a distinct nor in a non-distinct way, as the movement of water manifests itself in a whirlpool in the sea.


शिलान्तर्घनशुद्धैकचित्सारवपुर् आत्मवान् ।
सोम्यो व्यवहरन्न् एव तिष्ठत्य् उपलकोशवत् ॥11॥

11 A serene (somya) , self-controlled [man, whose] nature (vapus) is the pure, a spirit-essence (citsāra), [which is as] compact [as] in a stone 3 , continually actually participates in worldly activity like the inside of a stone (upalakośa) 4 .


अविभागघनस्पन्दा शिलान्तस् सन्निवेशवत् ।
अनानैव च नाना चिच् छिलान्तस् सन्निवेशवत् ॥12॥

12 The mind (cit) , [whose] movements are partially rigid, [exists] in a unitary way like the [solid] structure in a stone and in a manifold way like the [manifold] structure in a stone.


यद् अस्ति तच् चिति शिलाशरीरे सालभञ्जिका ।
यन् नास्ति तच् चिति शिलाशरीरे सालभञ्जिका ॥13॥

13 That which exists [is] in the mind (cit) : [the case of the chiseled]statue in the block of stone. What does not exist that [is] in the mind: [the case of the latent] statue in the block of stone.


भावाभावेषु यत् सत्यं चिन्मज्जौज्ज्वल्यम् एव तत् ।
मज्जसारा पदार्थश्रीस् तन्मयं स्यात् तद् एव हि ॥14॥

14 That which is real[is] in the forms of existence and non-existence is exclusively the Auﬂeuchten 5 of the mind-mark (cinmajjan) . Indeed, therichness of the objects [whose] essence is the [mind-mark] may be precisely this [auﬂeuchten]consisting of the [mind-mark].


पद्मं नानादिशब्दार्थांस् त्यक्त्वा यद्वच् छिलोदरम् ।
नाना तद्वद् इदं नाना तदतन्मयम् अद्वयम् ॥15॥

15 Just as a lotus[-shaped line]-leaving aside such word-meanings as "manifold"-as the inside of the stone [exists 1 but] appears in a manifold way, this [actually] unified world [exists] in a manifold way, [since it consists of it according to being], [but as appearance] does not [consist of it] 2 .


नानाप्य् एकतयानाना घनडिम्बशिलोदरम् ।
यथा तद् अविभागात्म तथेदं चिद्घनान्तरम् ॥16॥

16 Just as this interior of the stone, [which is] a compact body (d.imba) 3, [which is] partless [according to its] essence, [does not] actually [exist] in manifold ways because of [its] oneness, although [it] manifoldly [appears,exists] this interior of the mind-mass (cidghana).


यथामलः पयःकोशस् तलविश्रान्तभानुभाः ।
सन्न् एवासन्न् इवैवं चिन्नैबिड्यं सद् असत् त्व् इव ॥17॥

17 Just as a clear body of water 4 on [whose] surface the sun-glow rests [is] actually [there], apparently [however] is not [there], the mind-compactness (cinnaibid.ya) exists , but does not seem to exist.


यथा सोम्यपयोराशिकोटरं कलनोन्मुखम् ।
द्रवत्वात् स्पन्द्य् अथास्पन्दं तथेदं चिद्घनान्तरम् ॥18॥

18 Just as the interior of the calm sea, on the verge of a [movement] impulse, stirs because [it is] ﬂuid, and then [again] is motionless[is], this interior of the mind-mass (cidghana).


चिच्छिलाशङ्खपद्मौघस् तन्मयश् चाप्य् अतन्मयः ।
जगद् विद्धि सपद्मादिशब्दार्थं चिच्छिलान्तरम् ॥19॥

19 The many snail- and lotus-shaped lines in the mind-stone (cicchilā) consist of it [according to being] and do not consist of it [as distinct signs at the same time]. Know, [that] the world [is] the inside of the spirit-stone provided with the word meanings "lotus" etc.!


महाशिलाघनो ऽप्य् एष चिद्घनस् सुषिरोदरः ।
अरन्ध्रो ऽन्तश् चाद्वयो ऽच्छो जलान्तस् सन्निवेशवत् ॥20॥

20 This spirit-mass (cidghana) has a hollow 5 interior, although [it is] compact like a mighty rock, and [is] inside [at the same time] without gaps,uniform [and] clear 6 like the composition in water.


पततीदं जगद्ब्रह्म सोम्यम् अम्भ इव द्रुतेः ॥21॥

21 This world [called] brahman 1 tumbles [down] as [in itself] calm(somya) water [tumbles down] because [it] melts. 2


ब्रह्मणीदं सुषुप्ताभम् अस्त्य् अनन्यच् छिलाब्जवत् ।
मज्जबिल्वम् इव ब्रह्म जगद् ब्रह्मैव वामलम् ॥22॥

22 This deep-sleep-like world does not exist different [from brah-man ] in brahman , as a lotus[-shaped] line [does not exist different] from stone in stone. The brahman resembles a bilva[fruit] [composed of its] marrow 3[composed of its] marrow, or [in other words,] the world [is] nothingbut the spotless brahman.

यथा शून्यत्वम् आकाशे द्रवत्वं चाम्भसि स्थितम् ।
स्पन्दत्वं च यथा वायौ ब्रह्मणीदं तथा जगत् ॥23॥

23 Just as emptiness is present in space, and fluidity in water, and motility in wind, so [is] this world present in brahman [as having an intrinsic nature not distinct from it]. 4


ब्रह्मत्वं ब्रह्मणि यथा तथैवेदं जगत् स्थितम् ।
नानयोर् विद्यते भेदस् तरुपादपयोर् इव ॥24॥

24 Just as brahman-being, this world is present in brahman [as its own nature]; [there is] no difference between these twoas between [the two synonyms] taru and pādapa 5 .


यान्य् एतानि जगन्तीह तान्य् अन्त्राणि चिदाकृतेः ।
भावाभावादि नास्त्य् एषां तस्या इव कदाचन ॥25॥

25 [As for] these worlds here, [they are] the entrails of theGestalt of the spirit (cidākr.ti) . As for the [mind], [there is] never such a thing as existence and disappearance for these[worlds]. 6


ब्रह्मैव जगदाभासं मरुतापो यथा जलम् ।
ब्रह्मैवालोकनाच् छुद्धं भवत्य् अम्बु यथातपः ॥26॥

26 The appearance 7 of the world [is] nothing but brahman , as thewater [of a mirage is nothing but] heat in the desert. On account of [proper] contemplation, [the manifestation of the world] becomes nothing but pure brahman as [supposed] water becomes heat.


मेर्वादेस् तृणगुल्मादेश् चित्तादेर् जगतो ऽपि च ।
परमाणुविभागेन यद् रूपं तत् परं विदुः ॥27॥

27 The form that [results] from distinguishing the smallest particles(paramān.u) of [mountain] Meru, etc., the grasses, shrubs, etc., the den-ken (citta), etc., and also the world, is recognized as the absolute[form]. 1


तत्समूहस् तद् एवोच्चैश् चित्तमेरुतृणादिकम् ।
यत् सौक्ष्म्ये ऽपि हि सारात्म स्थौल्ये सारतरं हि तत् ॥28॥

28 The aggregation of these [particles is] quite the same [as their individual existence]. For something like the thought (citta), the [mountain] Meru, or a blade of grass, which is essence [of the mind] even [already] in the [state] of fineness, [is] in gross form a fortiori the essence [of the mind].


यथा रसात्मिका शक्तिः परमाणुतया तया ।
स्थिता जगत्पदार्थेषु पायसी ब्रह्मता तथा ॥29॥

29 Just as the force belonging to the fluid is present as such a smallest [invisible] particle in the objects of the world 2 ,[it is also the case with the] Brahman-being consisting of water 3 .


रसशक्तिर् यथोदेति तृणगुल्मतयाम्भसः ।
तथा नानातयोदेति सैवासेवेह ब्रह्मता ॥30॥

30 Just as the force of water [belonging to the] liquid [emerges] as grass or shrub, exclusively this Brahman -being emerges here [in this world] in manifold forms, as if [it] were not it [itself].


यैषा रूपविलासानाम् आलोकपरमाणुता ।
गुणगुण्यर्थसत्त्वात्मरूपिणी सा परात्मता ॥31॥

31 This being of the smallest particles of light for the manifestations of [colors and] forms: this [is] the being of the highest essence, [whose] actual form is the reality (sattva) of the [two] meanings "quality" and "quality carrier".



चितितत्त्वे ऽस्ति शैलादि तदभिव्यञ्जनात्मनि ।
पिञ्छपक्षौघकाठिन्यं मयूराण्डरसे यथा ॥32॥

32 Something like [for example] a mountain exists in the reality ofmind (cititattva) , [whose] nature [is] to make that [mountain, etc.] emerge, as the firmness of tail feathers and wings [exists] inPfaudotter 4 (mayūrān.d.arasa).



चितितत्त्वे ऽस्ति नानाता तदभिव्यञ्जनात्मनि ।
विचित्रपिञ्छिकापुञ्जो मयूराण्डरसे यथा ॥33॥

33 Multiplicity exists in the reality of mind (cititattva) ,[whose] nature [is] to make this [multiplicity] emerge,as diverse tail feathers [are present] in the pea adder.


नानानानात्मिके ह्य् एवं यथाण्डरसबर्हिते ।
विवेकदृष्ट्या दृश्येते तथा ब्रह्मजगत्स्थिती ॥34॥

34 In this way, just as the existence of the yolk (an. d.arasa) and the existence of the peacock (barhin), [both of which are] diverse and uniform [at the same time], are seen through by insight on the basis of judgment, so too is the existence of brahman and the existence of the world.



सनानातो ऽप्य् अनानातो यथाण्डरसबर्हिणः ।
अद्वैतद्वैतसत्तात्मा तथा ब्रह्मजगद्भ्रमः ॥35॥

35 The delusion of brahman and world, [which is based on the misinterpretation of] the being-of-unity and of a duality, in reality, [exists] as uniformly as [the delusion of] yolk and peacock, although [like the latter] it [seems to exist] in many ways.



यतस् सदसतोस् सत्ता सत्त्वताया यतस् स्थितिः ।
यतस् तदतदो रूपं भावं स्वं विद्धि तत् परम् ॥36॥

36 Know [that] this [is] the absolute own nature [from which] the being of being and not-being, from which the existence of reality-being[and] from which the form of this and of [something that is] not this [emerges]!



नानानानात्वम् अविदन् ननु नूनम् असम्भवम् ।
चिज्जगज्ज्वलनं पश्य बर्ह्यण्डरसबर्हिवत् ॥37॥

37 [O you who] do not understand the being [existing] [at the same time] in manifold and unified manner, [thinking]: "[This is] undoubtedly impossible": look at the shining of the spirit (cit) and the world [existing] in the manner of peacock-otter (barhyan.d.arasa) and peacock (bar-hin)!



यथा जगति चित्तत्त्वं चित्तत्त्वे च जगत् तथा ।
नानानानात्म चैकं च मयूराण्डरसो यथा ॥38॥

38 And as the reality of the spirit (cittattva) [is] in the world,the world [is] in the reality of the spirit. [It] is both [its] essence [at the same time] diverse and uniform and [exclusively] one like peacock and yolk.



नानापदार्थभ्रमपिञ्छपूर्णो जगन्मयूराण्डरसश् चिदात्मा ।
मयूररूपं त्व् अमयूरम् अत्र सत्तापदं विद्धि कुतो ऽस्ति भेदः ॥39॥


39 The yolk (an. d.arasa) of the peacock "world" (jaganmayūra) , [which is] spirit (cit) according to its essence, is full of [colorful] tail feathers,[namely] the delusion of manifold objects. But know, [that]in this [yolk] the state of being [contains the shape of a peacockand at the same time] [is] no peacock! From where [would a difference come into play here]?
